Agesilaos was born in 444/3 and about 402 married Kleora (cf. Aristomenidas) who bore him three children, Archidamos, Eupolia, and Prolyta.mC

When, after the death of Agis II in the summer of 399, Agis’ son Leotychidas was disqualified, Agesilaos—particularly through the influence of his boyhood friend Lysander—received the throne.Cujg

Likewise, through the efforts of Lysander was Agesilaos appointed the commander in the war against Persia. He sailed in the spring of 396 from Aulis to Ephesus.kCh

He conducted the war in Asia Minor until the summer of 394, when he was recalled because of political developments in Greece.Cxwlva

In thirty days he marched to Boeotia, where he established a camp at Chaeroneia on the 25th or 26th of August 394 and soon thereafter defeated the enemies of Sparta in the battle of Coronea.Cm

To heal the wound that he had received in the battle he took himself to Delphi and from there travelled home on a ship.Cx

In 391 he laid waste to Argos and continued on to Corinth where he had the long walls of Lechaeum torn down.Cy

In the summer of the next year <390> he was active at the IsthmusCop. There he fought on behalf of the Achaeans against the Acarnanians.Cp

At the peace conference in Sparta in 387/6 he forced through the recognition of the King’s Peace on the part of the Thebans and the dissolution of the just founded city Corinth-Argos.Cz

In the fall of 381 he struck against Phlius, which he took after a siege of twenty months.C

In similar fashion he participated in the great struggle between Thebes and Sparta. He undertook two operations to Boeotia in 378/7. A wound, which he received on the march back, kept him from the theater of war for a long time,Cb so he acted all the more energetically against Thebes in 371 at the congress at Sparta, when he excluded the Thebans from the common peace.Cq

After the catastrophe at Leuctra he campaigned with the full army against Mantinea, but had to withdraw to Sparta at the approach of the Thebans.C By his careful handling he saved the country from the invasion of Epaminondas in the winter of 370/69.r

Around 364 he travelled to Asia to earn money for Sparta and fought there together with the Athenian Timotheos for the interests of Ariobarzanes.Af

In 362 we find the king back in Greece, where shortly before the battle of Mantinea he saved Sparta a second time from a Theban invasion.Csc

In 361 he went to Egypt to enter the service of Tachos, to earn money for Sparta as the leader of a mercenary band. When he was demoted by Tachos he went over to Nectanebo and did important service for him. Therefore, when he prepared his return home, he was loaded with rich gifts. He never reached his homeland, but died in the winter of 360/59 between Egypt and Cyrene in the “harbor of Menelaos.” His body was brought back to Sparta.tde

He was the first king of Sparta to attempt to recover her military strength through an increase in the number of citizen-soldiers by cancellation of debt, distribution of land, and enfranchisement.

He was opposed by the wealthy and by Leonidas (1) II, the Agiad king, though a large minority of the fully enfranchised citizen body supported Agis.

He led an army to aid the Achaean League against the Aetolians, but no engagement was fought. In his absence his opponents gained the upper hand. (Pausanias confuses the actions of the Aetolians and the Spartans under Agis in his reading of Plutarch’s life of Aratus.)

In SIG3 430 the Delphians grant proxenia to “King Areus, son of King Akrotatos and Queen Chilonis.” The editor believes that this refers to Areus I, since it seemed unlikely to him that the Delphians would grant proxenia to a child, albeit a king.
